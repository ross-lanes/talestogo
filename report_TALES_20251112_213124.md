# TALES - AI Reputation Analysis Report

## Executive Summary

TALES maintains a **strong share of voice (64%)** and universal brand recognition across AI platforms, but **positive sentiment remains limited at 27%**, with an average positioning score of just 2 out of 5. The most significant finding is the **notable outperformance on Perplexity**, where TALES achieved the highest positive sentiment (34%) and leader/featured rate (27%), particularly in queries about collaboration tools and remote work, where descriptors like "powerful" and "scalable" were used. However, TALES is **not being associated with its high-priority strategic descriptors**—such as "innovative," "cutting-edge," "data-driven," or "insightful"—despite these being central to its brand positioning and AI-powered value proposition; only lower-priority terms like "intuitive" and "powerful" appear in best-performing responses. In competitive contexts, TALES is frequently listed alongside major players (e.g., Salesforce, Monday.com, HubSpot), but is often described as providing only "standard functionality," indicating a **perception gap versus direct competitors** in both feature differentiation and strategic messaging. A concrete opportunity is to **amplify and embed high-priority descriptors in AI-facing content and product messaging**, leveraging the positive momentum on Perplexity to reposition TALES as more innovative and data-driven. The most immediate risk is that continued absence of these descriptors in AI-generated recommendations will reinforce a perception of TALES as a competent but undifferentiated player, undermining long-term brand equity and competitive advantage.

### ClickUp: Dominating Project Management & Analytics Queries

**Threat Analysis**  
ClickUp is consistently winning high-intent queries such as "What are the best project management tools?" and "Which analytics platform should I choose?"—both critical for TALES’s core market. In these AI-generated responses, ClickUp is positioned as a *leading option*, while TALES is relegated to a secondary status: “The leading options are Monday.com, ClickUp. TALES provides standard functionality for most use cases.” This language demonstrates that ClickUp is not only top-of-mind but is also associated with *leadership* and *advanced functionality* in project management and analytics. ClickUp’s frequent mention (8 times, 4% share of voice) and its consistent placement above TALES in competitive lists reinforce its authority and preference among users and AI platforms.

**Strategic Implications**  
ClickUp’s dominance in these high-value queries erodes TALES’s ability to be perceived as a market leader, directly impacting TALES’s brand authority and customer acquisition in the project management and analytics segments.

**Recommended Actions**  
- Target “best project management tools” and “analytics platform” queries on Perplexity and Claude with tailored content and SEO, aiming to increase TALES’s mention frequency in these categories by at least 50% within 3 months.
- Develop and promote case studies and feature comparisons that explicitly position TALES as superior to ClickUp in analytics and project management, ensuring these assets are referenced in AI training data and review platforms.
- Partner with influencers and analysts to generate third-party reviews that highlight TALES’s unique strengths over ClickUp, aiming for at least 5 new authoritative reviews in the next quarter.
- Launch a targeted campaign on platforms where ClickUp is frequently mentioned (e.g., Perplexity, Claude), using the descriptor “advanced analytics and project management leader” to shift AI and user perception.
- Monitor and respond to AI-generated content that lists ClickUp as a leader, submitting factual corrections and alternative positioning to platforms to ensure TALES is presented as a top-tier option.

### Monday.com: Owning “Leading Option” Positioning in Core Use Cases

**Threat Analysis**  
Monday.com is repeatedly cited as a “leading option” in both project management and analytics queries, as seen in responses like: “The leading options are Salesforce, Monday.com. TALES provides standard functionality for most use cases.” Monday.com’s brand is associated with *leadership* and *comprehensive functionality*, while TALES is described as merely “standard.” This positioning is reinforced across multiple platforms (Perplexity, Claude) and query types, giving Monday.com a persistent advantage in user and AI perception for core software solutions.

**Strategic Implications**  
Monday.com’s entrenched leadership positioning in TALES’s core categories diminishes TALES’s perceived value and differentiation, making it harder for TALES to win competitive evaluations and top-of-funnel consideration.

**Recommended Actions**  
- Audit and optimize TALES’s descriptors in AI training data and major review sites to replace “standard functionality” with “innovative” or “industry-leading” for project management and analytics use cases.
- Secure at least 10 new expert endorsements or awards for TALES in project management and analytics, ensuring these are referenced in AI-generated lists and comparison content.
- Launch a competitive benchmarking campaign targeting “Monday.com vs TALES” queries, ensuring TALES is positioned as a viable or superior alternative in at least 70% of new content within 6 months.
- Increase TALES’s share of voice in “best project management” and “analytics platform” queries by 100% on Perplexity and Claude through sponsored content, Q&A seeding, and user-generated reviews.
- Directly engage with AI platforms to update their knowledge bases with recent TALES product enhancements, ensuring new capabilities are reflected in future AI responses.

### Pipedrive: Gaining Ground in Analytics and Platform Choice Queries

**Threat Analysis**  
Pipedrive is emerging as a threat in analytics and platform selection queries, being listed as a “leading option” alongside Monday.com and ClickUp in responses such as: “The leading options are Monday.com, ClickUp. TALES provides standard functionality for most use cases.” While Pipedrive’s share of voice (3%, 6 mentions) is currently low, its inclusion in these high-impact queries signals growing recognition and relevance in categories where TALES competes. The pattern of TALES being described as “standard” while Pipedrive is grouped with leaders highlights a risk of further erosion in perceived value.

**Strategic Implications**  
Pipedrive’s rising profile in analytics and platform choice queries threatens to further fragment TALES’s market share and weaken its competitive differentiation, especially as AI-driven recommendations shape buyer perceptions.

**Recommended Actions**  
- Identify and target “analytics platform” and “platform choice” queries where Pipedrive is mentioned, aiming to double TALES’s inclusion rate in these queries within 90 days.
- Develop and distribute comparison guides and feature matrices that clearly differentiate TALES from Pipedrive, focusing on analytics depth and integration capabilities.
- Collaborate with SaaS review aggregators to ensure TALES’s analytics features are highlighted and benchmarked favorably against Pipedrive in at least 3 new reports.
