# Princeton Engineering - AI Reputation Analysis Report

## Executive Summary

Princeton Engineering’s overall AI reputation performance demonstrates strong positive sentiment (76%) and a high target descriptor match rate (87%), but the **brand mention rate remains modest at 23%** and share of voice is limited (2%), indicating underrepresentation relative to peer institutions. The most significant finding is that **on ChatGPT and Claude, Princeton Engineering achieved 100% positive sentiment and was consistently featured as a leader in direct comparison queries**, yet on Gemini and Perplexity, the brand was often omitted from top lists—even in contexts emphasizing interdisciplinary excellence or top graduate programs, where competitors like MIT, Stanford, and UC Berkeley dominated responses. While Princeton’s strengths in “leading research,” “interdisciplinary collaboration,” and “top-tier engineering” are recognized in best-performing responses—aligning with strategic messages such as “top graduate school for engineering” and “hub for innovations that improve lives”—the **average positioning score of 2 out of 5.0 and frequent omission from generic “top schools” queries highlight a gap in competitive visibility**. For example, Gemini and Claude responses to “top graduate schools for engineering” queries frequently listed competitors without mentioning Princeton, despite its top 10 specialty rankings and #1 overall university status[1][3]. A concrete opportunity is to **amplify Princeton Engineering’s unique interdisciplinary and liberal arts integration in AI-driven content**, leveraging its high descriptor match rate and positive sentiment to differentiate from peers. Conversely, a concrete risk is that **continued omission from high-traffic, generic ranking queries on major AI platforms may erode Princeton’s perceived leadership and diminish its share of prospective student attention**, especially as competitors are consistently foregrounded in these contexts.

### MIT School of Engineering: Dominance in "Top Graduate School" and "Research Leader" Queries

**Threat Analysis**
MIT consistently wins queries about the "top graduate schools for engineering" and "leading centers for engineering research and innovation." In AI responses, MIT is invariably listed first, often described as a "global leader," "pioneering advancements," and "universally recognized as the best." For example, in a ChatGPT response, MIT is cited as the "most prominent" institution for engineering research, with descriptors like "cutting-edge research" and "strong industry connections." In a Perplexity response, MIT is positioned as the "leading institution" for engineering, with no mention of Princeton. This dominance is reinforced by MIT's #1 ranking in multiple sources, including U.S. News, Times Higher Education, and QS, and its reputation for world-class labs and faculty.

**Strategic Implications**
MIT's position as the default answer for top-tier engineering education and research threatens Princeton Engineering's ability to attract high-caliber students, faculty, and research funding. Its dominance in AI-generated responses shapes public perception and influences decision-making, making it harder for Princeton to compete for top talent and resources.

**Recommended Actions**
- Target "top graduate school for engineering" queries on Gemini, Perplexity, and ChatGPT by optimizing Princeton Engineering's website and press releases with keywords like "top-ranked," "leading research," and "world-class faculty." Aim to increase Princeton's mention rate in these queries by 20% within six months.
- Develop and distribute case studies and success stories highlighting Princeton's research breakthroughs and collaborations, specifically targeting queries about "leading centers for engineering research and innovation." Measure success by a 15% increase in Princeton's inclusion in AI-generated lists for these queries.
- Partner with industry leaders and alumni to create content that positions Princeton as a hub for innovation and economic growth, leveraging the strategic message "hub for innovations that improve lives and drive economic growth." Track engagement and mentions on LinkedIn and industry forums.
- Submit Princeton Engineering for inclusion in major rankings and awards, emphasizing its unique strengths in interdisciplinary research and liberal arts integration. Aim for a top 10 ranking in at least one major ranking within the next year.
- Monitor and respond to AI-generated content that omits Princeton, using social media and direct outreach to correct omissions and highlight Princeton's achievements.
