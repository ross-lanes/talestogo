## Executive Summary

TALES maintains a strong **brand presence** in AI-generated content, achieving a 100% mention rate and a leading 59% share of voice across 74 responses, yet sentiment remains mixed with only 30% positive and an average positioning score of 1.93 out of 5.0. The most significant finding is that while TALES is frequently recognized as a leader or featured brand—especially on Gemini (29% leader/featured) and Perplexity (25%)—the **association with core strategic descriptors such as "innovative," "cutting-edge," and "data-driven" is entirely absent**, despite these being high-priority brand goals. In contrast, competitors like HubSpot, Zoom, Salesforce, and Monday.com are often cited as leading options in direct comparison queries, with TALES typically described as providing only "standard functionality" rather than differentiated value. Notably, positive descriptors such as "powerful" and "intuitive" do appear in best-performing responses (e.g., Gemini: "intuitive interface"; Perplexity: "powerful functionality"), but these are medium-priority terms and occur infrequently. The clearest opportunity is to **amplify and embed high-priority descriptors** in AI-facing content and product messaging to close the gap between strategic intent and market perception. The primary risk is that continued lack of alignment with target descriptors will reinforce a perception of TALES as a generic or undifferentiated player, especially as competitors are more consistently recognized for leadership and innovation in the same contexts.
