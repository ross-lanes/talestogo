# Princeton Engineering - AI Reputation Analysis Report

## Executive Summary

Princeton Engineering’s AI reputation performance is characterized by **high positive sentiment (90%) and a strong match to target descriptors (73%)**, but suffers from **low brand mention rate (25%) and limited share of voice (2%)**, indicating underrepresentation in key AI-generated content. The most significant finding is that **Princeton Engineering is frequently omitted from top-tier engineering school lists on major platforms (e.g., Gemini, Claude, Perplexity) in response to queries about leading or interdisciplinary engineering institutions**, with competitors like MIT, Stanford, and Berkeley consistently dominating these responses. While the brand’s strategic messages—such as being a "top graduate school for engineering" and a "leading research institution"—are occasionally reflected (notably in Perplexity, where Princeton was featured in 50% of mentions), **critical high-priority descriptors like "groundbreaking discoveries" and "global connections" are rarely or never associated with the brand**. In competitive context, Princeton is sometimes included alongside peers in direct comparison queries, but is often listed after more prominent institutions and lacks consistent leadership positioning, as seen in Gemini and Claude responses where it is mentioned but not featured. A concrete opportunity lies in **amplifying Princeton Engineering’s unique interdisciplinary and transformative impact narrative, which is under-leveraged despite strong alignment with institutional strengths**; conversely, the primary risk is **continued omission from AI-generated rankings and discussions, which could erode visibility and perceived prestige among prospective students and stakeholders**.
